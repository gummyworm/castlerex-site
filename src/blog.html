Moving

Part I

Well it's moving day...again. I have moved (at least once) every single year since graduating high school. But this...THIS...is the biggest one yet. Not in terms of possesions. Per usual, I have lined up rubber maid tubs like a small cargo train ready to be escorted right out the door, and I hope the whole process will take no more than an hour. Nay, this move is big because instead of ping-ponging around Iowa I will be taking a one-way flight...somewhere else.

Yeah, Somewhere. Presently, I'm seriously considering moving to New York City. The Big Apple. If I do, I will absolutely not be bringing any vintage computers or electronic supplies along. This is not just because I won't have room for them, but because the very idea of owning things in big city environmet, one filled with bugs and general filth, brings me great anxiety. In a way, that is part of what draws me toward that city. I will have no choice but to adopt, for my own sanity, a sort of minimalist lifestyle.

Minimalism appeals to me. It's hard not to see the allure. No possesions to drag you down. The freedom to not worry about looking for, taking care of, or keeping inventory of one's possesions. Mate, if there's a way to live that is it. Moving has also made me confront the sheer number of clothes I own. This has made me revisit the idea of a "social uniform". An idea I first heard about through Steve Jobs, and first took to heart through a conversation with a friend a couple years back.

For me, the appeal it is not just that it eliminates the need to choose what to wear. Actually, that's not even part of the appeal. I like that there is absolutely no reason to own, say, 20 different shirts. At some level, fashion does interest me, so it is easy to justify owning 20 different shirts when each one is only fit for certain occasions or as part of a specific outfit. With a social uniform, the only test a shirt needs to pass to determine whether to keep or discard it is its condition.

Anyway, huge digression...let's move on

Besides NYC, allow me to muse on another prospective move. I could move back to Springfield, MO, a city that I truly love despite its consistent ranking as one of the worst cities in America. It is one of the cheapest places to live in the entire US, and I could easily buy a nice LOFT =) (I love lofts) or house where I could pursue all sorts of creative material endeavors. Having a hardware lab would be an attainable dream. Easily attainable. But, though few things are more enjoyable than warming up that soldering iron and digging through disorderly bins of wires and IC's, I know that on some level these things aren't making me happy- long term, that is.

At this very moment, I'm kinda torn by these two very opposite destinations. One of the most expensive cities in America versus one of the cheapest. Owning a beautiful space or throwing away twice as much to rent a freaking broom closet. It's not just some "lifestyle" pulling me to NYC. It's just so far outside my comfort zone and so demanding on my perspective of how to live. That isn't to say the lifestyle does not appeal to me. Competition breeds quality, and the necessity to fight for one's survival in the most populous city in the USA has objectively produced a city that is world class in every imaginable way: fashion, art, cuisine, nightlife, name it...That would be, if nothing else, neat to experience.

I've kind of given myself two extremes here...There are other options. Denver would be a logical place to move. But, well, we'll see..

Even as I've typed this my conviction to at least visit NYC has grown. If I don't fall in love, so be it. I can't see myself being there forever. But I don't think it's an experience I should deprive myself of either.

Part II

Ahhh, well, the hard part is over. All my possesions have been packed and are out of sight and mind for the time being. What a relief.

Having everything gone already makes me feel better about the move. With the first hurdle cleared, I can set my eyes on the next. Soooo, this move...what do I take...I will be flying, which greatly limits what possesions I can carry. My desktop (iMac) is clearly out. As are both my guitars. Realistically, I could take maybe two suitcases and a backpack. But, let's use this opportunity to REALLY go minimalist. I'll take just one suitcase and one backpack. This is what I took to Israel, and it was plenty sufficient for a month long trip. I never missed anything, so it is definitely enough. In each vessel I will pack the following:

Backpack:

  • Laptop (Macbook Air)
  • Work Laptop (Macbook Pro 2015)
  • Laptop charger
  • Phone (USB-C) charger
  • Kindle
  • micro USB cable
  • mp3 player
  • mouse
  • headphones
  • passport
  • glasses
  • sunglasses
  • Nintendo Switch
  • Nintendo Switch games
  • Nintendo 3DS
  • Nintendo 3DS games
  • The Way of Kings (book I'm reading)
  • Grooming supplies: toothbrush, brush, razer, nail clippers.
  • razer charger
  • Contact solution, toothpaste
  • Contacts

Suitcase:

  • 1 pair jeans
  • 4 pairs shorts
  • light jacket
  • jean jacket
  • 8 shirts
  • socks/underwear
  • shoes
  • athletic wear:2 shorts + 2 shirts
  • swim trunks

Apologies if this is neither helpful or interesting. In my mind, writing this has helped me visualize what I need to pack. Off the plane, I will Uber to an AirBnB somewhere. I haven't decided exactly where to stay yet, but I am strongly leaning toward somewhere in Brooklyn. Currently the top neighborhoods I'm considering are Bushwick and Williamsburg. Bushwick has the cost advantage; Williamsburg- location.

Part III

Well..I'm here. I flew from Minneapolis to Brooklyn on Monday. I didn't take any vacation, so I've been working all week, and I am quite exhausted. I've been staying in Williamsburg, near the Marcy station stop, which I like quite a bit, but as I write this I am in Bushwick, where I think I will likely try to live. I've met a couple people searching for a roommate- we'll see if anything pans out, but otherwise I've requested another AirBnB between Bushwick and Bedstuy to get me through another week. I brought almost exactly what I listed above (though I forgot my contacts...), so I'm packin' very light. Despite the tiring nature of trying to fix my homelessness while working fulltime, I've had fun so far! And the bagels here are AMAZING!

Part IV

Okay, here we go...I'm at my second AirBnB now. Had a pretty decent day, but I'm feeling the exhaustion of this homelessness. I am in the BedStuy area now, which is alright. I applied for a room in Bushwick right near one of my new favorite coffee shops in the area: Dweebs. I've been there three times now. It's also half a mile from another coffee shop I've become quite fond of: Caffeine Underground. It has a kind of vapor wave vibe going on, which is quite unique and interesting. I've been spending some time working on Castle Rex while at the cafes, which is giving me something to occupy time and stay busy. I can't cook at this AirBnB, and I haven't really wanted to, but in an attempt to save money I had pop tarts and cookies for supper. Every other meal was bagels, which I see no problem with! Probably not the best diet to sustain in the long term... Hopefully my next entry will conclude the "moving" log and I will officially be transitioned to "moved". Welp, tomorrow comes quick. Another day at the office!

Part V

And we're done. I've completed my move to Bushwick. The first night was a bit rough. I had a few drinks, which made it tolerable, but it was quite cold without some basic household essentials (a blanket). I am still sleeping on a yoga mat and probably will for the forseeable future. It's not that bad and I feel like over time it will be better for me. It makes sleeping in far less tempting and just feels natural. I just LOVE how few posessions I have now. I feel like I can truly just, at any time, leave my apartment with the clothes on my back and never return. It is absolutely liberating! Going forward I will keep fighting to keep my possesions to an absolute minimum to maintain that ability.

I did make a run to Target in the LES yesterday, so the blanket/pillow situation is better now. Another thin blanket wouldn't hurt.

So...I guess that's it! Thus concludes the Moving saga. =)
